3 Types of Roofing Materials You Need To Know About

Your roof is more than just a finishing touch on your home or business – it’s an investment in the safety, comfort, and longevity of your property. A good roof not only protects your building from the elements, but also offers peace of mind, while adding to your home’s curb appeal and overall value. That’s why choosing the right types of roofing materials is one of the most consequential decisions a property owner can make. At Titan Roofing, we understand the value of the right types of roofing materials and are dedicated to helping our clients make the best decision for their needs, budget and style.

When it comes to selecting the best roof for your home or business in Arizona, there are several options to choose from. The individual types of roofing materials have their own unique pros and cons, so it's important to consider your specific taste and needs before making a decision. Here, we'll break down three popular types of roofing materials available in Arizona: tile, foam, and shingle. Read on for more!

Tile Roofs: A Classic Choice for Arizona Homes

Tile roofs are a classic and popular choice out of the three types of roofing materials for Arizona homes. Not only do they look beautiful, but they are also durable and long-lasting. Tile roofs are typically made from clay or concrete, and they are available in a variety of colors and styles to match your home's architecture. They are particularly well-suited to the hot Arizona climate, as they can withstand extreme temperatures and harsh UV rays.

One of the biggest advantages of tile roofs is their longevity. With proper maintenance and care, tile roofs can last up to 50 years or more. They are also fire-resistant, which is particularly important in areas of Arizona prone to wildfires. Additionally, tile roofs can help to keep your home cool during the searing summer months, which can help to reduce your energy bills.

Foam Roofs: A Cost-Effective and Energy-Efficient Option

Foam roofs are a cost-effective and energy-efficient option for Arizona homes. They are made from a type of plastic called polyurethane, which is sprayed onto the roof as a liquid and then expands and hardens into a solid foam. Foam roofs are particularly well-suited to flat or low-slope roofs, which are common in our part of the country.

One of the biggest advantages of foam roofs is their energy efficiency. Foam roofs provide excellent insulation, which can help to keep your home cool during the blazing Arizona summers and warm during the cooler winter months. This can help to reduce your energy bills and make your home comfortable year-round.

Foam roofs are also lightweight, which can help to reduce stress on your home's structure. They are highly durable and can last up to 30 years or more with proper maintenance and care. Additionally, foam roofs are waterproof, which can help to prevent leaks and water damage to your home.

Shingle Roofs: A Versatile and Affordable Option

Shingle roofs are a versatile and affordable option of the three types of roofing materials for Arizona homes. They are made from a variety of materials, including asphalt, wood, and metal, and are available in an amazing range of colors and styles. Shingle roofs are also particularly well-suited to sloped roofs, which are common in this neck of the woods.

One of the biggest advantages of shingle roofs is their affordability. Shingle roofs are typically less expensive than tile or foam roofs, making them a great option for homeowners on a budget. Additionally, shingle roofs are relatively easy to install and maintain, which can help you to keep your costs down over time.

Shingle roofs are also celebrated for their versatility. They can be customized to match your home's architecture and design, and they are available in a broad range of colors and styles to suit your personal preferences. Additionally, shingle roofs are highly durable and can last up to 30 years or more with thorough maintenance and care.

Deciding Which Types of Roofing Materials are Right for You?

Ultimately, deciding the best types of roofing materials for your home or business in Arizona will depend on your style, needs, and preferences. It’s also important to consider the climate and weather patterns in your specific area of Arizona when selecting a new roof. For example, if you live in an area prone to heavy rainfall or monsoon season, you may want to choose a roofing material that is particularly waterproof and can withstand high winds.

Another factor to consider is the overall style and design of your home or business. Whichever types of roofing materials you want to choose always ensure that it complements and enhances the look of your property, while also providing the necesssary protection and durability. Some property owners will also need to consider with HOA requirements.

Generally speaking, if you're looking for a classic and durable option, tile roofs are a good bet. For those that prioritize an energy-efficient and cost-effective option, foam roofs are a smart idea. And if you're looking for a versatile and affordable option, shingle roofs are a great choice.

No matter which roofing option your choose, it's important to work with a reputable, reliable and experienced roofing contractor. A well-regarded professional roofing contractor can help you to select the best roofing option for you and ensure that it is installed properly to ensure the longevity and durability of your new roof.
